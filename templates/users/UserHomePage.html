Understanding Dementia

What is Dementia?

Not a single disease, but a group of brain disorders causing progressive decline in cognitive function, interfering with daily activities and independence.

Common Symptoms

Memory loss, confusion, communication difficulty, personality changes, and impaired reasoning or decision-making.

Causes & Types

Alzheimer's is the most common type. Other causes include vascular damage, Lewy bodies, and frontotemporal degeneration.

Key Prediction Features

MMSE — cognitive test score · CDR — dementia rating · nWBV — brain volume · eTIV — intracranial volume
